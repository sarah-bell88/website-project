Summary:

Jason was Batman's second Robin. Originally, his backstory was nearly identical to Dick Graysons. But after the Crisis on Infinite Earths event, his backstory was re-worked significantly. He went from being from the circus to being a poor kid living on the streets of Gotham. His initial run-in with Batman involved successfully stealing 3 out of 4 tires on the Batmobile.

However, Jason was angrier and more reckless than Dick, and he had his fair share of detractors. In 1988, DC famously ran the Death in the Family event, where readers were allowed to vote on if Jason survived. It's rumored that one caller spammed the voting phone line, tipping the vote towards his death.

In the mid-2000s, Judd Winnick decided to revive the character, using a recent event to justify how Jason could suddenly be alive. He then wrote the Under the Red Hood and Lost Days stories to much acclaim, and Jason was remade as the Red Hood.
